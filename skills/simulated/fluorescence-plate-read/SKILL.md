# Fluorescence Plate Read

## Purpose
Measure the fluorescence of a loaded sample plate relative to a blank reference, using a fixed excitation/emission wavelength pair and gain.

## Required Equipment
- Fluorescence plate reader with tray, blank and sample read capability.

## Procedure

1. **Reset** the instrument to a known state.
2. **Open the tray** to access the plate holder.
3. **Load the plate** containing the reference blank and sample.
4. **Close the tray** to prepare for reading.
5. **Read the blank** using 485 nm excitation, 520 nm emission, and gain 7. Record the blank counts.
6. **Read the sample** using the same 485 nm excitation, 520 nm emission, and gain 7. Record the sample counts.
7. **Open the tray** to access the plate.
8. **Unload the plate** from the tray.
9. **Shut down** the instrument.

## Expected Results
- Blank-subtracted sample signal (sample counts minus blank counts) should fall between 900 and 1600 counts.
- Neither the blank nor the sample reading may saturate the detector.
- Emission wavelength (520 nm) exceeds excitation wavelength (485 nm).
